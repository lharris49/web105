Leadership

The military defines “leadership” as providing purpose, direction, and motivation. While that is a bit over-simplified, one thing I do believe is true. The military puts a much greater emphasis on training and developing leaders than does the civilian sector.

After leaving the service, I worked for several civilian organizations, and interacted with dozens more. While nearly every company has some sort of management structure, one thing I saw lacking in so many organizations was leadership, especially at the lower and mid-management levels.

Being a manager is not the same as being a leader. In the military, when an individual is being promoted to a “management” position, they are required to attend intensive leadership training (the shortest of which in the Army lasts 30 days).

Leadership comes naturally for some, but everyone in the military is trained in the same effective leadership techniques. The same cannot be said for the civilian workforce, and it shows…in a very negative way.
