Civilization 6: From the PC to PS4

Civilization began as a board game in the early 1980’s, but when Sid Meier translated it into a computer game in 1991, all hell broke loose. Mothers lost their children; wives lost their husbands. The common mantra of “just one more turn” turned many a gamer’s late nights into early mornings. One of the most intoxicatingly addictive games ever, Civilization has been captivating video game enthusiasts for decades. But until recently, that enthusiasm was reserved for the PC gamer. At the end of 2019, game developer Firaxis finally released the latest version, Civilization 6, for PS4 and X-Box One. But how would this legendary game translate to the console? Well, let’s take a look!

Still a Beauty to Behold

Your typical (elitist?) PC gamer might tell you converting Civilization to the console just won’t work. It will never look the same. And you know what, they’re right. The visuals on console systems will never compare to the high-end graphic cards and high-res monitors available to PC gamers. But lucky for us, Civilization has never been a game that thrived largely on it’s visuals. It is a strategy game. Even so, the look of Civ 6 on the PS4 is still a beauty to behold. The wonders are wonderous, the animations are visually impressive, and the game board looks about like what any experienced Civ player would expect. It may not be as detailed down to the pixel, but Civ 6 is still lovely on the console. And the supporting elements give this game a spectacular and homey feel…elements like the music.

The Sound and the Fury

The Civilization series has always been known for its outstanding score. Christopher Tin (Civ 4) is back to continue that tradition. His main theme song for Civ 4, “Baba Yetu”, is legendary. It inspired countless memes and became the first video-game inspired song to win a Grammy award in 2011.

His main theme song for Civ 6, “Sogno di Volare” is equally as dramatic and mood-evoking as his previous efforts. The in-game score adds substance and content to the game playing experience as well. Each civilization has its own sub-set of themes that progress throughout the game. I couldn’t help but feel a sense of pride as my Canadian armies thrashed the Romans as an orchestral version of “Oh Canada” swelled in the background.

“I predict it will join [Christopher Tin’s] previous Civ effort in the pantheon of the greatest pieces of music written for a videogame” – T.J. Hafer, PC Gamer

But the aural pleasures of Civ 6 are extend beyond than just the score. The sound effects add a sense of realism and depth to the game. When a volcano erupts, you can almost feel the rumble for the next few turns. If you’re focused on a coastal hex, you can hear the gentle lapping of the waves on shore. And if you’re one of those players that prefer to set your options to “quick combat”, you’re really missing out. There’s nothing like the mechanical and explosive sounds of a Giant Death Robot scorching the souls of your enemies. When it comes to ear candy, Civ 6 on the console brings the whole bag of tricks.

Something Feels Funny

The one area where the console version is somewhat awkward is in the game feel. Controlling your units with a game pad is less convenient than with a mouse and keyboard. Rather than pointing and clicking where you want them to go, you must drag the selection icon to the desired hex with the joystick. This is especially cumbersome if you want to move your aircraft carrier halfway around the map. But with some practice, I was able to overcome this minor inconvenience and it took little away from the game playing experience.

I mean, it is still a turn-based game so there is no rush in selecting exactly where I want a unit to head. The hexes are still hexes, and the same frustration of being just one hex too far from that coal resource is ever present.

Final Verdict

Civilization 6 is another outstanding entry to the Civ genre, and it translates well to the console version. While the controls may take a little getting used to, halfway through the first play through, you will be so immersed in the strategy and sounds of the game that you won’t care. If you’re a PC gamer, you can stick with your version of the game. But for those of us that lean towards console gaming, you’re in luck! One of the greatest time evaporating games is now at your fingertips. Kiss your loved ones goodbye, because you won’t see them again until you finally finish the Pyramids wonder or you wipe out the last of those pesky Greeks. Civilization 6 for the PS4 is a worthy compliment to it’s PC brother, and I give is 4.5 out of 5 stars.
