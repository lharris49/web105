wanderlust (noun): strong longing for or impulse toward wandering or travel

I think my “wanderlust” began my sophomore year of high school when I started taking French. I had a great teacher, and he really inspired me to see more of the world. It only increased my junior year when I made a new good friend, Reinhart Schmidt, who was a German foreign exchange student.

As our junior year was winding down, he invited me to come to Germany and stay with his family for a while. I reached back out to my French teacher (who was also a travel agent) to ask what that might cost. He suggested that I should not only visit my friend, but join a European tour that he and another language teacher chaperoned each year. I talked with my parents and they agreed to help me pay for the trip. That Summer I spent a month and a half touring around Europe, and I was forever infected with the travel bug.

“With age, comes wisdom. With travel, comes understanding.”

– Sandra Lake, Author

During my 20’s, my father was often employed managing manufacturing plants throughout South East Asia. I was fortunate that the company he worked for would pay to fly family members over to visit, so I got my first taste of Asian cultures. From Thailand to Singapore to Malaysia, it amazed me how different they were from each other. Their uniqueness made each visit a fascinating experience for me.

Of course, once I joined the military, the number of countries on my “visited” list exploded! My first assignment was in Japan, then onto Panama, followed by Italy. And in each stop, I ventured to many of the neighboring countries as well…and sometimes even further. When I was stationed in South Korea, for example, I went on a jaunt to New Zealand and Australia. I have been lucky to have seen and experienced some many cultures in my lifetime, but I think the wanderlust has been exhausted. I enjoyed each visit, but now I am what I call “wander weary”. I still like making short excursions, but I think my world travelling days are done.
